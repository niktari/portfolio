Sunshield

Identity, Design, Packaging

2021

Sunshield is a hypothetical skincare brand that carries a wide range of sunscreen products: lip balms, face creams, serums, body lotion, and more. To account for the various product lines, a package generator was created to ensure that the packaging fits within Sunshield's design system, no matter the size of the product.

Applying a design system to differently shaped and sized vehicles can often be a manual, tedious process. But with an algorithm that's designed with certain restrictions in mind, there's an opportunity for the packaging to generate itself.

This is especially important when Sunshield relies on its packaging to break through the clutter of sunscreen brands. Sunshield has a fun and energetic visual language consisting of shield motifs and vibrant colors. Masking effects are also a predominant element of the design system since, in essence, that's what sunscreen does—masks and protects. The package also includes a sleeve where the wordmark wraps around the edges of the box, thereby acting as a shield itself.

About

Nikki (Niktari) Makagiansar is a designer who likes to code based in Brooklyn, New York. She enjoys combining motion, print, code, and typography to create bespoke visual systems and generative identities.

Nikki is a recent graduate of Parsons School of Design, where she was also a research assistant for the Design & Technology director, Justin Bakse.

She is also a co-founder of SPICY, a grassroots arts and publishing organization for and by queer and trans people of color. Prior to pursuing a design practice, she was part of the brand marketing team at Comedy Central.
