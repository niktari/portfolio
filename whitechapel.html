Whitechapel

Identity, Design, Development

2020

A generative wordmark was developed as part of a hypothetical identity refresh of Whitechapel Gallery, a modern and contemporary art gallery based in East London since 1901.

In the spirit of the gallery, the refresh fuses the new with the old: a bespoke pixel-based serif typeface was created to pay homage to the gallery's original, historical identity, while the mosaic motif inspired by the last rebrand from the London-based agency Spin was also incorporated. The salmon and terracotta colors are an additional reference to Whitechapel's older branding collateral.

The word "gallery" was also decidedly dropped from its name to emphasize its longstanding presence within East London. The identity comes in various mosaic weights and is made purely, tediously, and lovingly with code.

About

Nikki (Niktari) Makagiansar is a designer who likes to code based in Brooklyn, New York. She enjoys combining motion, print, code, and typography to create bespoke visual systems and generative identities.

Nikki is a recent graduate of Parsons School of Design, where she was also a research assistant for the Design & Technology director, Justin Bakse.

She is also a co-founder of SPICY, a grassroots arts and publishing organization for and by queer and trans people of color. Prior to pursuing a design practice, she was part of the brand marketing team at Comedy Central.
