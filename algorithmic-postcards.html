Algorithmic Postcards

Design, Print

2021

These are a series of similar yet personalized postcards that were both computationally and manually generated.

An algorithm was used to generate shapes that resemble echinoderms, also known as starfish, sea urchin, and sand dollars.

The number of letters in each recipient’s name was used as a data input to inform the number of sides that each echinoderm-like shape should have. For instance, a recipient with eight letters in their name would result in a shape with eight sides.

This data, coupled with an algorithm created with certain restrictions, afforded each postcard to be uniquely personal yet still fall within the same design system.

About

Nikki (Niktari) Makagiansar is a designer who likes to code based in Brooklyn, New York. She enjoys combining motion, print, code, and typography to create bespoke visual systems and generative identities.

Nikki is a recent graduate of Parsons School of Design, where she was also a research assistant for the Design & Technology director, Justin Bakse.

She is also a co-founder of SPICY, a grassroots arts and publishing organization for and by queer and trans people of color. Prior to pursuing a design practice, she was part of the brand marketing team at Comedy Central.
