Walk for Me

Design, Typesetting

2020

The Wikipedia page for "Ball culture" → was translated into a 70-page, purely typographic book. Ballroom, or ball culture, is an underground scene that was pioneered by queer Black and brown youth in Harlem around the 1970s. Participants "walk" or compete in categories that reflect, and at times, subvert mainstream and popular culture.

Given the history of ball culture and its references to, but more notably, influence within high fashion, the typographic language of the book emulates spreads from Harper’s Bazaar, specifically the typographic styles of Alexey Brodovitch and Fabien Baron.

About

Nikki (Niktari) Makagiansar is a designer who likes to code based in Brooklyn, New York. She is currently developing data-driven and algorithmic identities at Pentagram under Giorgia Lupi.

Nikki is a graduate of Parsons School of Design, where she was also a research assistant for the Design & Technology director, Justin Bakse.

She is also a co-founder of SPICY, a grassroots arts and publishing organization for and by queer and trans people of color. Prior to pursuing a design practice, she was part of the brand marketing team at Comedy Central.
